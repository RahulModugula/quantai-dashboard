## CapStructureAgent brief

LEVERAGE: $550M gross debt / $6.7M FY22 EBITDA = **82.1x** (pre-restructuring). Post-TSA: $450M 1L + $125M new 2L = $575M on unchanged TTM EBITDA = **85.8x**. Both measurements are functionally meaningless — this is a cap-stack-in-distress, not a going-concern levered business. The question is what EBITDA does forward, not backward.

COVERAGE: LTM EBITDA / cash interest. Cash interest pre-TSA at ~SOFR+725 on $500M 1L ≈ **$61M**; LTM EBITDA $6.7M → **0.11x coverage**. Broken by any definition. Post-TSA: 1L cash interest falls to ~$49M (on reduced $400M 1L principal), 2L is PIK so no cash interest; total cash interest **~$49M** vs FY23 guided EBITDA of $25-35M → coverage improves to **0.5-0.7x**. Still negative free cash flow but survivable if revolver capacity holds.

FULCRUM: **The new 2L PIK Convertible is the fulcrum security by design.** It sits senior to $165M of Series A Preferred and junior to $450M of 1L debt. In any re-distress scenario, the 2L converts to the majority of reorganized equity (standard Chapter 11 cramdown mechanics). HPS controls the 1L and would be the natural senior creditor in any Chapter 11; the 2L participant group controls what comes out the other side. This is the correct layer for a loan-to-own thesis.

RECOVERY ANALYSIS:
- Base case ($30M EBITDA by FY2024): PT wage inflation normalizes per BLS trajectory. Company trades to ~7x EBITDA = $210M enterprise value at exit. 1L recovers at par; 2L converts to ~80% of post-reorg equity = ~$150M on $125M face (accreted to ~$145M by conversion) → **~105c par recovery** on a 30-month hold.
- Bear case ($10-15M EBITDA): Sector wage pressure persists; re-enters Chapter 11 in late 2024. Liquidation / going-concern sale at distressed multiple (~5x trough EBITDA = $60M EV). 1L takes $400M face against $60M value → ~15c par. 2L takes control of residual equity via cramdown; residual value after 1L claim ≈ $0, but 2L owns post-reorg equity on a re-emergence plan → **~55-70c par recovery** (highly scenario-dependent; meaningful impairment but not zero).
- Bull case ($50M+ EBITDA by FY2025): Sector consolidation thesis plays out. Strategic buyer (Select Medical, Athletico, or a PE roll-up platform) pays 11-13x EBITDA = $550-650M. 1L recovers at par; 2L converts at favorable valuation → **~250-320c par recovery** on a 24-36 month hold. This is the realized scenario (August 2025 take-private closed at $523.3M TEV, ~11.2x LTM Adj EBITDA).

ASSET COVERAGE: Outpatient PT is asset-light — leased clinics, intangible clinician relationships, modest PP&E ($65M at Q4'22). Tangible asset coverage of the $450M 1L is approximately **14%**. This is emphatically **not** an asset-backed recovery thesis; it is a cash-flow / going-concern / optionality thesis. The Workers' Compensation franchise (legacy since 1996) is the only quasi-asset — sticky payor relationships in IL/TX/PA — but not collateralizable.

BEST RISK/REWARD TRANCHE: **The new 2L PIK Convertible** — not even close. The 1L is already owned by HPS at par and returns are capped by rate spread. The Series A Preferred sits below the new 2L and has no conversion optionality against the true operating recovery. The 2L is the only tranche where the investor underwrites the outcome (wage normalization) and captures the majority of the upside.

SUMMARY: The post-TSA cap stack is structurally unsustainable without EBITDA recovery, but the PIK coupon on the 2L converts the coverage problem from a cash problem into an accretion problem — buying 18-24 months of runway. The 2L PIK Convertible is the correctly-sized entry into an 86x-leveraged stack with a visible catalyst path.

## SituationAgent brief

KEY STRUCTURAL EVENTS:
- **2022 EBITDA collapse (-83%)** from $39.8M → $6.7M — driven by PT wage inflation and therapist attrition, not demand destruction. Clinic visit volumes held; the company simply couldn't staff enough therapists to capture them. This is a **supply-side** problem, which has a different (faster) resolution path than a demand-side thesis.
- **Feb 2022 HPS-led $550M refinancing** baked in aggressive leverage assumptions (~14x trailing, priced for continued COVID-era margin). When FY2022 margin compressed, covenants broke immediately — this is not a secular decline story, it is a refinancing that was mispriced 12 months earlier.
- **April 2023 TSA is a lender-friendly restructuring** engineered by HPS and prospective 2L participants. It preserves the first-lien seniority for HPS, exchanges $100M of that 1L into the 2L PIK convertible (de-leveraging the cash-pay stack without impairing secured rights), and adds $25M of new money. Existing equity is not wiped — the 1:50 reverse split preserves NYSE listing. This structure signals that **the senior creditors believe in the going concern**.

UPCOMING CATALYSTS:
- **Q2 2023 earnings (expected Aug 2023)** — first look at whether EBITDA run-rate exits single-digits. Even a $15M quarterly run-rate validates the supply-side recovery thesis.
- **Reverse stock split effective June 14, 2023** — administrative, but maintains NYSE listing compliance.
- **BLS PT wage growth data (monthly)** — the macro input. Q1 2023 prints already show deceleration; continuation through Q2-Q3 is the primary thesis validation.
- **Q3/Q4 2023 EBITDA prints** — 2-quarter window to re-rate. If EBITDA confirms $25-30M run-rate, paper trades to 90c+ and optionality rises; if it confirms sub-$15M, second distress event likely and we exercise fulcrum conversion.
- **Sector M&A activity** — Select Medical, Athletico, and US Physical Therapy consolidation signaling improves exit optionality.

NOISE: SPAC-era securities class action (FVAC II / 2021 de-SPAC disclosures) — expected to be resolved under D&O insurance and sponsor indemnities. Will distract management but should not impair post-TSA capital. **FLAG: at TSA signing this is unresolved; settled for $31M in Sep 2024.**

INFORMATION GAPS:

- Monthly same-clinic visit volume — reported quarterly only; PT recovery is a monthly signal.
- Exact 2L PIK convertible terms: conversion price, change-of-control premium, anti-dilution formula — must be verified against final indenture, not TSA summary.
- Advent International's behavior on the Series A Preferred in any future restructuring — historically supportive but not guaranteed.

SUMMARY: Supply-side (PT labor) problem in a structurally growing sector ($53B market, fragmented); entry is being offered at peak stress and is visibly catalyzed by dateable EBITDA prints. This is a classic "EBITDA recovery trade" — 2-3 quarters of data will vindicate or invalidate the thesis with high information density.

## CreditRiskAgent brief

VERDICT: PROCEED WITH CAUTION
RISK RATING: 4 (high)

CHALLENGES TO RECOVERY MATH:
- The base case ($30M EBITDA by FY24) assumes linear wage normalization. Real PT wage data is regional and noisy — a 2-quarter delay would trigger either a covenant amendment request or re-entry to formal restructuring. The cap-structure brief's "105c par" base-case recovery assumes a clean conversion path; more realistically, a 6-month delay compounds PIK accretion but also compounds senior interest, eroding the equity value we would own.
- "Equity-conversion upside" assumes we get to convert at participant-favorable valuations. In practice, HPS (senior) has leverage to force a sale at a level where the 2L recovery is capped (they only need par + cash interest to be whole). If HPS's senior cash needs force a sale at $350M EV in late 2024, 2L recovery would be closer to 60-70c par, not 105.
- PIK-convertible-to-equity transitions are **slow in mark-to-market terms**. Audit committees holding this paper will mark at book value minus a 15-25% haircut for 18-24 months even if the credit improves. The return profile looks lumpy on realized-return basis but has stale NAV volatility for any IRR-sensitive LP.

TAIL RISKS:
- **CMS reimbursement cut**: Medicare Physician Fee Schedule review every 3 years. The 2024 cycle proposed a ~3.4% cut to PT codes before partial reversal. A steeper cut would compress EBITDA by $5-8M — enough to break the recovery thesis.
- **Illinois Workers' Comp reform** — ATI's largest state exposure, and IL workers' comp is under periodic legislative scrutiny. A structural change to fee schedules or authorization rules would disproportionately hit ATI's margin-accretive segment.
- **Competitive consolidation from above**: Select Medical or UnitedHealth/Optum acquires a distressed peer (USPH, Professional PT) at a lower multiple before ATI's recovery is complete — tightens M&A exit multiples.
- **SPAC litigation settlement exceeds $50M** with insufficient D&O coverage — impairs junior capital including our 2L.

LEGAL / PROCESS RISKS:
- TSA is **out-of-court**, which means no 363-sale protection for assets and no Bankruptcy Code stay if creditors defect. If Q3 2023 shows deterioration, a Chapter 11 filing re-opens the entire cap stack — our 2L PIK becomes a pre-petition claim subject to valuation fights and cramdown process.
- HPS's 1L has roughly 85-90% coverage under going-concern value assumptions. In a foreclosure scenario they can credit-bid; our 2L recovery in that path depends entirely on cramdown equity value (see bear case above).
- Appraisal-rights risk in any future take-private: minority shareholders can force valuation litigation. (Note: ultimately arose August 2025 in minimal form — short-form merger squeezed out ~1.4% minority; appraisal exposure was bounded.)

EXIT LIQUIDITY: Three paths:
1. **PIK converts to equity on re-distress event** → we hold majority post-reorg equity → M&A exit to strategic (Select Medical, Athletico) — this is the base path for distressed credit and played out in Aug 2025.
2. **Whole-company sale without conversion** (Select Medical / UnitedHealth/Optum pays par+) → cleaner, faster, less likely given HPS's own incentives.
3. **Secondary market for 2L PIK paper** — illiquid, 1-2 dealers, 3-5 point bid/ask. Effectively mark-to-matrix until a catalyst.

Primary risk: M&A market for outpatient PT chains cooled meaningfully from 2021 peak. 2023-2024 M&A activity will be thinner than the thesis assumes; exit timing is a real concern.

SUMMARY: The trade has real merit and the structure is well-designed, but the recovery math glosses over execution timing and HPS's divergent senior incentives. Size as a meaningful-but-bounded position. If Q3 2023 EBITDA print (Nov 2023) does not confirm $25M+ run-rate, reassess; do not add.

## Executive Summary
ATI Physical Therapy is a top-5 US outpatient PT platform (~900 clinics, 24 states) in a $53B fragmented market. FY22 EBITDA collapsed 83% (to $6.7M) on a **supply-side** shock — PT wage inflation and therapist attrition — not demand destruction. The April 2023 TSA creates a new 2L PIK convertible ($25M new money + $100M 1L-to-2L exchange, 8% PIK, Aug 2028 maturity) that is engineered to be the fulcrum security. We should participate: the instrument gives us asymmetric upside on PT wage normalization (which BLS data already shows beginning) and downside protection via fulcrum-conversion into the majority of post-reorg equity if the situation re-distresses. Asset coverage is thin (asset-light operating model), so this is explicitly a cash-flow / going-concern / optionality trade, not an asset-backed one — and our sizing reflects that.

## Thesis
- **Supply-side shocks resolve faster than demand-side shocks.** PT clinic visit demand is secular; the 2022 EBITDA collapse was labor inability to capture demand. BLS Q1 2023 already shows PT wage growth decelerating. Base case EBITDA recovery to $25-35M by FY24 is measurable within 2-3 quarterly prints.
- **The instrument is a fulcrum by design.** HPS and the prospective 2L participant group engineered the TSA to own the equity if Q2-Q4 2023 disappoints. This is loan-to-own from day one — downside is controlled by the same structure that delivers the upside.
- **Workers' comp franchise is an underappreciated margin differentiator.** ATI's workers' comp business (legacy since 1996, concentrated in IL/TX/PA) delivers structurally higher margin than pure-play PT competitors. Public-side sell-side coverage treats it as undifferentiated — this is an information-edge source for our underwriting.
- **Sector consolidation optionality is free.** Top-6 chains are ~10% of US PT clinics. Post-restructuring ATI, with our capital, becomes a consolidator in a market where every smaller operator is facing the same wage pressure. This roll-up optionality has real value in the base and bull cases and costs us nothing at underwriting.

## Downside
Bear case recovery is 55-70c per dollar on our 2L face (see cap-structure and risk briefs). On a 1.5% AUM position ($18M on $1.2B fund), the capital at risk is **45-80 bps of fund NAV** in a realistic bear scenario. This is consistent with our capital-preservation philosophy: if the thesis is wrong (PT wages don't normalize by Q3 2023, CMS cuts reimbursement, or IL workers' comp reform hits), we absorb a bounded, non-catastrophic loss. Structural protection: (i) PIK coupon converts the coverage shortfall into an accretion problem, buying 18-24 months of runway; (ii) fulcrum-conversion mechanics give us majority control in any downside restructuring; (iii) the 1L-to-2L exchange reduces our structurally senior pari-passu debt by $100M from day one — a meaningful de-risking event baked into the TSA itself.

## Sizing Rationale
Initial 1.0-1.5% AUM reflects high conviction on the supply-side thesis, medium conviction on execution timing. Scale to 2.0% if the Q3 2023 print (Nov 2023) confirms $25M+ quarterly EBITDA run-rate. Hard cap at 2.0% — this is an illiquid, junior, single-issuer trade and portfolio theory demands bounded concentration even at high conviction. Optional hedge: 25%-sized short in USPH (US Physical Therapy, $500M cap, liquid pure-play comp) as insurance against a sector-wide PT wage shock. Not required in base case; activate if Q2 2023 print suggests wage pressure persisting.
